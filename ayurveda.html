What is Ayurveda?

Ayur + Veda

Ayur = Life     Veda = Knowledge

The Science of Life

Ayurveda is an ancient Indian system of medicine that focuses on maintaining health through the balance of mind, body, and spirit. This 5,000-year-old holistic approach emphasizes prevention and treatment through lifestyle practices, dietary guidelines, herbal remedies, and spiritual wellness.

Unlike modern medicine that often treats symptoms, Ayurveda addresses the root cause of imbalance and works to restore harmony between the individual and their environment.

Balance

The Five Elements (Pancha Mahabhuta)

According to Ayurveda, everything in the universe is composed of five fundamental elements

Earth (Prithvi)

Represents stability, structure, and the solid aspects of matter. Provides foundation and support.

Water (Jal)

Symbolizes flow, cohesion, and the liquid aspects. Essential for all life processes and emotions.

Fire (Agni)

Represents transformation, heat, and energy. Governs digestion, metabolism, and inner radiance.

Air (Vayu)

Embodies movement, breath, and circulation. Controls all bodily movements and nervous system.

Ether (Akash)

The space element, representing consciousness and communication. Creates room for all other elements.

The Three Doshas

The doshas are biological energies found throughout the human body and mind. They govern all physical and mental processes.

Vata Dosha

Ether + Air

Characteristics:

Dryness Lightness Coldness Roughness Subtlety Mobility

Vata governs movement, circulation, breathing, and nervous system function. People with dominant Vata tend to be creative, energetic, and quick-thinking, but may experience anxiety, restlessness, and irregular digestion when imbalanced.

Pitta Dosha

Fire + Water

Characteristics:

Heat Sharpness Lightness Liquidity Slight Oiliness Intensity

Pitta controls digestion, metabolism, and body temperature. Pitta-dominant individuals are typically focused, ambitious, and intelligent, but may experience anger, inflammation, and digestive issues when out of balance.

Kapha Dosha

Earth + Water

Characteristics:

Heaviness Slowness Coolness Oiliness Smoothness Stability

Kapha provides structure, stability, and immunity to the body. Kapha-predominant people are typically calm, loving, and stable, but may experience sluggishness, weight gain, and congestion when imbalanced.

Yoga: Union of Body, Mind & Spirit

Yoga, derived from the Sanskrit word "Yuj" meaning "to unite," is a comprehensive system that harmonizes physical postures, breathing techniques, meditation, and ethical principles to achieve optimal health and spiritual growth.

Benefits of Regular Yoga Practice:

  • Improves flexibility and strength
  • Reduces stress and anxiety
  • Enhances mental clarity and focus
  • Balances the nervous system
  • Promotes better sleep quality
  • Supports cardiovascular health
